Java Concurrency: Thread Confinement

Most concurrency problems occur only when we want to share a mutable variable, or mutable state, between threads. One way to avoid this problem is to simply not share the data between the threads. This technique is known as thread confinement and is one of the simplest ways of achieving thread safety in our application.
